Video Production

This project required the creation of an artistic digital production using Microsoft MovieMaker or iMovie (or in my case, Canva). Instead of live video footage, still image production embracing a theme ("What Is an Algorithm?") were modified using techniques such as zooms, and pans that dissolve, fade, and wipe into one another.

What Is an Algorithm?

\

My video is an informational piece titled "What Is an Algorithm?" which is directly based on an "unplugged" activity from my 7th-grade Computer Science unit plan, "Block Busters: Building and Fixing Our First App.". The video uses the "peanut butter and jelly sandwich" analogy to explain why computers require precise, step-by-step instructions.

Here is a summary of the media and manipulations I performed to create this video:

  • Software Used: I used Canva's video editor (in Standard Mode) to assemble and edit the project.
  • Audio Production:
    • Narration: I recorded my own voice-over narration using Audacity and exported it as a high-quality .WAV file.
    • Background Music: I used the royalty-free track "Equatorial Complex" by Kevin MacLeod (incompetech.com), as required by the license.
    • Audio Mixing: In Canva, I placed the narration and music on separate tracks and significantly lowered the volume of the music track to ensure the narration was clear and complemented by the music, rather than being overpowered by it.
  • Visual Media:
    • Images: I used 17 high-resolution still images sourced from Pexels and iStockPhoto.
    • Video: I also integrated one short, live-action video clip (the father and son) to enhance the PB&J analogy.
  • Editing Manipulations & Effects (per the rubric):
    • Formal Title & Credits: The video begins with a formal title screen and ends with a "Thank You" and credits screen that provides full attribution for both the music and image sources.
    • Screen Captions: I used numerous on-screen text captions to reinforce the narration and highlight key concepts (e.g., "You want a 'sandwich'?", "What Do I Do? Hmm...", "An Algorithm!!!").
    • Panning & Zooming: I applied the Ken Burns effect (using Canva's "Animate" feature) to several images. Two clear examples are the slow zoom-in on the "spreading peanut butter" image and the pan/zoom effect on the "student on the stairs" image.
    • Transitions: I used multiple Dissolve, Fade, and Match & Move transitions to move smoothly between different visual clips throughout the video.
    • Other Video Effects: I applied several other effects, including:
      1. Overlays/Opacity: Using blended/faded overlays, such as the sandwich appearing over the math background.
      2. Ghosting/Echo: Applying an echo effect to the "student on the stairs" image to create a sense of motion.
      3. Blur/Flash: Using blur and flash effects on the Scratch code images [0:35] for visual emphasis.

The final video was rendered as an .mp4 file with a total runtime of 2:26.
